How many bitcoin places are there of each category?

Places listed on bitcoinmap.cash, coinector.app or inside the respective Android Apps are verified, categorized and tagged by products/services. Each place has been individually reviewed and we only list the most famous places which are also being promoted on Google Maps. We think that if a place wants to be listed on bmap.app he should understand the basics of online marketing which is listing their business on Google Maps, its like a free website with massive outreach. We also support merchants in registering their place on Google, so that they can be more succesfull long term. No matter if someone likes Google or not, their product Google Maps is being shipped on all Android devices, so leveraging that network effect to promote places that accept Cryptocurrencies is an opportunity that should not be missed.

Hopefully in the future we can also provide statistics about the amount of customers these places are having in each coin as that would be much more meaningful to measure the success of a brand strategy. So this statistic about the amount of places can not be interpretated as a scoreboard of success at all, it could mean the opposite.

Where are these crypto merchants located?

Merchant adoption in Venezuela and Slovenia is leading the race of numbers go up, while in Australia there has been a very focused BCH community ever since 2018 in Australia, BCH City North Queensland, that has achieved to create a circulating economy. Also there has been strong adoption on carribean islands lately and rumours about a circulating BCH economy in St.Maarten, St. Kitts & Nevis have arised. Swiss, Japan, Colombia and Argentina are some other hotspots apart from the classics like New Hampshire (USA), Arnhem (Holland), Rovereto (Italy) and Barcelona (Spain).

The latest adoption in El Salvador has largely happened in an isolated environment inside the governments own wallet that requires KYC, also the Bitcoin Beach wallet is a custodial solution which we do not count as a p2p bitcoin user as transactions inside custodial wallets do not happen on-chain and are undocumented, usually custodians abuse the trust of their clients and lend out their money to speculate with that risking the clients funds to their own profit. There has also been heavy promotion of company specific apps that are incompatible to the original protocol of Bitcoin. We are happy to list any merchant that accepts crypto on-chain but in El Salvador that is not happening. All statistics have proven that the amount of transactions has stagnated and has even gone down for BTC during 2022. But I am sure the real bitcoiners in El Salvador are aware of that issue and will provide data of places that accept Bitcoin on-chain p2p as soon as it is available.
